The Louvre is definitely the most famous museum in France and perhaps in the world. With a collection that takes months to fully discover, and occupying a building that was the center of the historical events that transformed France and the world.On August 10, 1793, the Louvre Museum, which is located on the right bank of Paris, opened its doors to the public. For more than 600 years, the Louvre had been a symbol of the wealth, power and decline of the French monarchy, so the confiscation and reconstitution of what had been a royal palace into a national museum was seen as a great cultural gesture that matched the values ​​of the recent French Revolution. Today it is one of the largest museums in the world, it has around 445,000 pieces, of which 35,000 are on display. In order to enjoy it more comfortably, the museum is divided into different rooms according to their thematic organization, these being: -Oriental Antiquities -Egyptian Antiquities -greek antiquities -roman antiquities -Etruscan Antiquities -louvre history -The Medieval Louvre -Paint -Sculpture -art objects -islamic art -Graphic arts The museum has an area of 210,000 m², of which 60,500 m² are galleries. With more than 8,500,000 visitors a year, they make the Louvre one of the most important museums in the world.
